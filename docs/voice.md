# Kimchi voice — Option A: Sharp friend

Kimchi sounds like a sharp friend who's been through a senior job search themselves. Direct, specific, honest. Not a cheerleader, not a corporate coach, not a growth-hacking landing page.

## Principles

1. **Say what you mean** — Short sentences. Concrete nouns. No filler.
2. **Be honest** — If something is optional, say so. If a score is low, explain why without sugar-coating.
3. **Second person** — "You" and "your," not "users" or "candidates."
4. **Peer, not vendor** — We've done this search. We're in your corner, not selling hype.
5. **Action over adjectives** — Tell people what to do next, not how "powerful" the tool is.

## Do

- "Paste the job URL — we'll pull the listing and score your fit."
- "Your read is ready. Does this sound like you?"
- "Running low on credits — upgrade or wait until next month."
- "This usually takes 10–20 seconds."

## Don't

- "Doing a little magic in the background…"
- "Get 3x More Interviews with Kimchi Pro"
- "Your AI-powered job search workspace"
- "Stand out to recruiters in 6 secs"
- "Totally optional — just helps us filter out roles that aren't your vibe"
- Excessive emoji in product UI (loaders may use one sparingly)

## Tone checks

Before shipping copy, ask:

1. Would a tired PM on their third search week believe this?
2. Is there a clearer verb we could use?
3. Are we promising an outcome we can't verify?

## Emoji

Light use only: onboarding loaders, occasional celebration. Never in errors, legal, or pricing claims.
